Introduction

This appendix provides detailed information on the usability evaluation metrics, task instructions, survey questionnaire, and flow diagrams used in the study.

Usability Evaluation Metrics

Definition of Metrics

Awareness

Awareness refers to the extent to which the users can effectively perceive the information displayed on the privacy controls. It demonstrates the usability of privacy controls through factors such as visibility and the ease of locating and accessing displayed information. This attribute encompasses two key features, which are location and display type.

  • Location: The placement of privacy controls on a website plays a significant role in determining user awareness and the visibility of privacy-related content. When privacy controls are prominently displayed, users are more likely to notice and interact with them. Conversely, if privacy controls are not strategically positioned, there is a higher chance that users will overlook them. For example, a privacy notice positioned at the top of the screen would garner greater attention compared to one placed in the bottom-left corner.
  • Display Type: The display type includes the layout formats and visual characteristics of privacy controls. Effective display types such as the pop-up window help users quickly grasp the content of the privacy control and enhance their awareness of the privacy practices employed by the website. This aspect is particularly crucial for privacy nudges and privacy notices, as they often have different display formats compared to privacy policies, which are typically presented as plain text only.

Efficiency

Efficiency refers to the measurement of the time and effort required for users to access their desired privacy controls. When evaluating privacy settings, for instance, this attribute considers the time it takes for users to locate and modify their privacy settings, as well as the overall intuitiveness of the process. This attribute includes one key element, which is the number of clicks required for accessing the privacy control.

  • Number of clicks: The number of clicks correlates with the ease of accessing the privacy information and settings. By reducing the number of clicks, the user experience can be significantly improved as it simplifies the navigation process and saves time for the user.

Comprehension

The comprehension aspect of usability for privacy controls, particularly for privacy policies and settings, pertains to the extent to which users can understand and interpret the information provided. Comprehension encompasses two primary features: language and the presence of a table of contents.

  • Language: The language focuses on the readability and clarity of the text used in privacy controls. It emphasizes that the information presented should be easily understandable by a diverse audience. To achieve this, websites should provide the option to translate privacy control content into multiple languages.
  • Table of contents: A table of contents refers to the inclusion of a structured and organised outline within privacy policies or settings. Typically, it consists of hyperlinks that enable users to easily navigate to specific subsections within the text. The presence of a table of contents serves as a valuable tool for users to swiftly grasp the sections and topics covered in lengthy texts and quickly access the information they are interested in.

Functionality

Functionality assesses the extent to which a privacy control encompasses various categories of privacy content. It measures the types of privacy data that are covered or disclosed. For instance, a properly designed and functional privacy notice should provide information about a wide range of privacy aspects and the types of data that are collected.

  • Type of privacy content: The type of privacy content refers to the breadth of privacy data topics addressed by privacy controls, including areas such as advertisements, cookies, email notifications, and other relevant aspects. A high coverage of these privacy content types ensures that information about the website's privacy data collection practices is effectively communicated to users.

Choice

In addition, we consider the influence of dark patterns on the usability of privacy controls through the choice attribute. This attribute evaluates whether the websites provide meaningful options and clear guidance for users to manage their privacy preferences. It covers two crucial features: the types of options available to users, and the choice guidance provided.

  • Types of options: In privacy controls such as nudges, users should be presented with options to exercise control over their privacy preferences. These options may include accepting or declining all data-sharing practices, as well as managing certain aspects of their own privacy preferences.
  • Explicit choice guidance: Clear choice guidance involves concise steps and actionable instructions to explain each option provided in privacy controls. It is essential for users to understand the implications of their choices and make informed privacy decisions.
  • Nudging Patterns: Nudging patterns refer to the design elements of a privacy choice interface that influence users to select certain options over others, sometimes utilizing dark patterns to encourage less privacy-protective decisions. Dark patterns are increasingly used to coerce users into accepting default or less secure privacy settings. One prevalent example is the use of a privacy nudge for cookie selection, where the design of the interface guides users to make specific choices.

Task Instructions

Tasks Description

Task Instructions

You will interact with 4 website prototypes in the following categories: health, e-commerce, news, and finance. After completion of all tasks, you will be presented with a post-task questionnaire.

Now you will visit a prototype of a health website. Please click on continue when you are ready to proceed.

Booking a doctor appointment: Please spend around 5 minutes to navigate the Medical Clinic website. Your objective is to book a free doctor appointment using the website's interface.

Registration: If you would like to register on the website, please register with any fabricated email address you prefer, e.g., testregister@gmail.com. Click Continue when you have completed the interaction with this website.

Survey Questionnaire

Questions

Italicized text is used to indicate survey flow. Answer choices are shown in bullets below each question.
